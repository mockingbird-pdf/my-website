Photograph 51

The image that changed science forever.

Photograph 51 was a famous X-ray diffraction image that helped reveal the structure of DNA. It was taken in 1952 at Kings' College London by British chemist and crystallographer Rosalind Franklin and her PhD student Raymond Gosling. Photograph 51 was developed over 62 hours by exposing strands of deoxyribonucleic acid to x-rays.

What is X-Ray Crystallography?

X-Ray crystallography is a technique used to find the exact 3D atomic structure within a molecule. This is done by sending x-rays at crystallised samples. The atoms scatter the rays and this creates a diffraction pattern. This is then analysed to map the molecule's structure.
